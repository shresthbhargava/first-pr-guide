# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

This is a space for beginners. That means people here are learning, asking questions that might seem basic, and making mistakes. That's the point.

We are committed to making participation in this project a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or gender identity.

## What We Expect

**Be kind.** Especially to beginners. Everyone starts somewhere.

**Be constructive.** Feedback should help people improve, not make them feel bad.

**Be patient.** Maintainers are volunteers. Contributors might be in different time zones or dealing with life.

**Be honest.** If something is wrong, say so — but say it respectfully.

**Give credit.** Acknowledge others' ideas and contributions.

## What We Won't Tolerate

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks of any kind
- Dismissing a contribution because it's "too small" or "trivial"
- Gatekeeping — making people feel like they don't belong
- Spam PRs made purely for program points with no genuine effort
- Sexualized language or imagery
- Publishing others' private information without consent

## Enforcement

Violations will result in:
1. A warning
2. Temporary ban from the repository
3. Permanent ban for repeated or severe violations

To report a violation, open a private issue or contact the maintainer directly via GitHub.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — issues, PRs, discussions, and any other community interaction connected to this repository.
